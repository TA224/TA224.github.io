Privacy Policy for Lock-in

Effective Date: March 14, 2026

Overview

Lock-in ("we", "our", or "the extension") helps you improve your focus by blocking distracting websites. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information Lock-in accesses, how it is used, and your control over your data.

Data Collection and Storage

Local Data Only

All data collected by Lock-in is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage.local API. No data is ever transmitted to external servers owned or operated by us.

Lock-in stores:

  • Blocked sites list – Websites you choose to block
  • Focus mode schedules – Your configured focus sessions
  • Timer settings – Your preferred focus and break durations
  • Strict mode passcode – Optional passcode to prevent disabling Lock-in

What Lock-in Accesses

To block distracting websites, Lock-in requires these Chrome permissions:

  • All websites (<all_urls>) – To detect when you visit a blocked site
  • Tabs and browsing activity – To check open tabs against your block list
  • Web requests – To intercept and block attempts to load restricted sites
  • Scripting – To show focus reminders on blocked pages
  • Storage – To save your settings locally

Third-Party Resources

Lock-in loads styling and icons from:

  • Boxicons – via unpkg.com
  • Materialize CSS – via cdnjs.cloudflare.com

These CDNs may log your IP address and browser information according to their own privacy policies:

  • Cloudflare Privacy Policy
  • Unpkg Privacy Policy

No personal data from your Lock-in settings is shared with these services.

No Sale of Personal Information

We do not and will never sell your personal information to advertising platforms, data brokers, or information resellers.

How Your Data Is Used

Your locally stored data is used only to:

  • Enforce your site blocking preferences
  • Activate focus mode according to your schedule
  • Maintain your timer settings
  • Verify your strict mode passcode when needed

Data Sharing

We do not:

  • Transmit your data to external servers
  • Use analytics or tracking tools
  • Share data with advertisers or third parties
  • Collect browsing history beyond checking against your block list

Your Rights and Control

You have full control:

  • View/Modify – All settings in the Lock-in popup
  • Delete – Uninstalling Lock-in automatically removes all data
  • Reset – Clear all settings through the extension interface

Children's Privacy

Lock-in is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has provided information through Lock-in, please contact us.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

If this policy is updated, the "Effective Date" at the top will be revised. Continued use of Lock-in after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
